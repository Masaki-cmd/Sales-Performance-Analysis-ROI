# Sales-Performance-Analysis-ROI
## Summary:

### Data Analysis Key Findings

*   A significant data quality issue was identified, with 17.93% of total net sales and 32.82% of total net quantity categorized as "Unknown" for product types, hindering accurate analysis. Addressing this issue has an estimated Return on Investment (ROI) of 1,379.05%.
*   The "Online Store" sales channel exhibits substantial untapped growth potential, currently showing significantly lower net sales compared to "Point of Sale." Investing in online channel activation and enhancement offers the highest estimated ROI of 2,845.74%.
*   After re-evaluating returns as a reducible cost, optimizing discounts and returns strategies was found to be viable, with an estimated ROI of 53.50%.
*   "Point of Sale" (POS) remains the primary sales channel, contributing the vast majority of revenue, which may indicate an over-reliance on traditional sales methods.

### Insights or Next Steps

*   Prioritize investment in "Online Channel Activation and Enhancement" due to its exceptional ROI of 2,845.74%, focusing on improving user experience, digital marketing, and streamlining the online purchase process.
*   Implement a "Comprehensive Data Quality Improvement Program" as a high priority (1,379.05% ROI) to accurately classify "Unknown" products, establish robust data governance, and enhance overall decision-making.
